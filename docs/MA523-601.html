Course Description

(3 credit hours) Vector spaces, linear transformations and matrices, orthogonality, orthogonal transformations with emphasis on rotations and reflections, matrix norms, projectors, least squares, generalized inverses, definite matrices, singular values.

Prerequisites. An undergraduate linear algebra class, such as MA305 or MA405.

Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to master the following:

  • The mathematical concepts of matrices and vectors, and their basic operations (inner product, transpose, scalar multiplication, addition, multiplication, and outer product), and their time complexity.
  • The mathematical problems of solving linear systems and least squares problems, and the existence and uniqueness of their solutions.
  • The fundamental matrix decompositions (pivoted LU, Cholesky, QR), and their use in the solution of linear systems and least squares problems.
  • The Singular Value decomposition (SVD), and its use for the solution of least squares problems and low- rank approximation of matrices.
  • Generation and manipulation of matrices in Matlab, and the fundamental Matlab commands.
  • The properties of floating point arithmetic and its effect on the accuracy of matrix operations.
  • The concept of sensitivity of a mathematical problem to perturbations (conditioning), and the behavior of matrix operations in floating point arithmetic (numerical stability).

List of Topics

Here are the major topics of MA 523, with the approximate regular-semester time allocated to each:

  • Module 0: Introduction to Matlab and Computational Setup (~1 week). Major topics include Matlab onramp and basic Matlab usage.
  • Module 1: Vectors, Matrices, and Matrix Operations (~3 weeks). Major topics include vectors and matrices, basic matrix operations, matrix-vector products, matrix multiplication, inverses, and special matrices.
  • Module 2: Floating Point Arithmetic and Numerical Representation (~2 weeks). Major topics include toy floating point systems and IEEE double precision arithmetic.
  • Module 3: Linear Systems, Norms, and Conditioning (~2.5 weeks). Major topics include linear systems and sensitivity, vector and matrix norms, conditioning of linear systems, and numerical linear solvers.
  • Module 4: Singular Value Decomposition and Applications (~3 weeks). Major topics include foundations of the SVD, square and special matrices, rank, norms, optimality, distance from singularity, applications to statistics and PCA.
  • Module 5: Least Squares and Data Fitting (~1.5 weeks). Major topics include least squares problems, data fitting, column spaces, and regularization.
